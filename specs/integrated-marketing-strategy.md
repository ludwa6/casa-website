## Executive Summary

Casa Vale da Lama faces a unique challenge: launching both a physical property and its digital presence simultaneously while the property is still under construction. This integrated strategy turns that constraint into a **storytelling advantage** by building anticipation through a phased reveal campaign across social media and web platforms.

The strategy leverages the "transformation journey" narrative - showing the property's evolution from construction to completed eco-resort - to build audience investment before the April 1st launch.

---

## Strategic Alignment: Website ‚Üî Social Media

### The Core Challenge
- **Physical Reality:** Earth-moving machines, unfinished rooms, ongoing landscaping
- **Digital Need:** Visually compelling website inspired by Quinta Marugo's polished aesthetic
- **Solution:** Phased reveal strategy where social media builds anticipation while website showcases the vision

### The Opportunity
**Turn construction into content:** Before/after transformation content performs exceptionally well on social media and builds authentic connection. The construction phase becomes proof of the eco-resort's authentic, hands-on development.

## Content Creation Workflows

### Pre-Launch (Dec-March): Cati's Documentation System

**Filming Protocol** (from Social Manager's guide):
- **Steady shots:** Two hands, slow movement
- **Duration:** 5-7 seconds per clip
- **Timing:** Morning or late afternoon (best light)
- **Orientation:** Vertical (Instagram Reels priority)
- **Angles:** Wide, medium, close-up (3 of everything)
- **Repetition:** Same shots at different stages (transformation content)

**Organization:**
- Shared folder structure (Dropbox/Google Drive)
  - /Construction_Progress (dated folders)
  - /Rooms (by room type)
  - /Restaurant
  - /Farm
  - /Landscape
  - /Details (materials, textures, design elements)
  - /Team
  - /Drone

**Handoff to Social Manager:**
- Weekly content review sessions
- Social manager selects clips for editing
- Feedback loop: what content needs capturing next

## Cross-Platform Content Strategy

### Content Repurposing Framework

**Single Asset ‚Üí Multiple Formats:**

Example: Professional room photoshoot
- **Website:** High-res gallery (10-15 images per room)
- **Instagram Feed:** Best 2-3 images, carousel post
- **Instagram Reels:** 7-second walk-through video
- **Instagram Stories:** Behind-scenes of photoshoot
- **Email Newsletter:** Featured room of the month
- **Blog Post:** "Designing Our Eco Rooms" with photos

### Platform-Specific Optimization

**Website (WebFlow):**
- Optimized for SEO (schema markup, alt text, meta descriptions)
- Comprehensive information (detailed, factual)
- Professional tone
- Desktop + mobile optimized
- Conversion-focused (booking CTAs)

**Instagram (Primary Social Platform):**
- Optimized for engagement (hooks, trends, hashtags)
- Emotional, inspirational content
- Conversational tone
- Mobile-first (vertical video)
- Community-focused (interaction, DMs)

**Potential Future Platforms:**
- **TikTok:** If Instagram Reels perform well, expand to TikTok for younger audience
- **YouTube:** Long-form content (property tour, sustainability deep-dives)
- **Pinterest:** Design inspiration, Algarve travel guides (high-intent traffic)

## Brand Voice & Messaging Consistency

### Tone Across All Platforms:
- **Authentic & Grounded** (not overly polished/corporate)
- **Passionate about Sustainability** (educational but not preachy)
- **Warm & Welcoming** (personal, not transactional)
- **Connected to Place** (Algarve, local culture, land)

### Key Messages (Unified):
1. **Eco-Luxury is Possible** (comfort + sustainability)
2. **Regenerative, Not Just Sustainable** (actively improving the land)
3. **Farm-to-Table Reality** (not just a buzzword)
4. **Algarve Beyond the Beach** (authentic regional experience)
5. **Slow Living, Deep Connection** (wellness, nature, community)

### Avoid:
- Greenwashing language (vague "eco" claims without specifics)
- Over-promising (manage expectations during soft launch)
- Sterile hotel-speak (humanize the brand)

## Risk Management & Contingency Plans

### Risk: Construction Delays Push Opening Past April 1
**Mitigation:**
- Build flexibility into social messaging ("Opening Spring 2025" vs. "April 1")
- Have backup content ready (continue transformation narrative)
- Pivot influencer visits to post-opening if needed
- Maintain website as "coming soon" until firm opening date

### Risk: Content Quality Insufficient (Unfinished Property)
**Mitigation:**
- Focus on storytelling over perfection (authenticity > polish)
- Use strategic framing (highlight finished areas)
- Lean into "work in progress" narrative as intentional
- Supplement with Algarve landscape/lifestyle content

### Risk: Low Social Media Engagement Pre-Launch
**Mitigation:**
- Invest in early paid promotion (small budget to jumpstart)
- Engage more aggressively with Algarve/eco travel communities
- Collaborate with local businesses for cross-promotion
- Adjust content strategy based on analytics (double down on what works)

### Risk: Influencers Don't Deliver Expected Results
**Mitigation:**
- Diversify influencer portfolio (don't rely on 1-2 big names)
- Set clear deliverables in collaboration agreements
- Review content before public posting
- Build in contract clauses for non-performance

### Risk: Booking System Integration Delayed
**Mitigation:**
- Launch website with email/phone booking option
- Manual booking management initially
- Set expectations ("Booking opening soon" messaging)
- Prioritize system integration but don't block website launch
